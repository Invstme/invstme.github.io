Investors

Some Topics We Are Frequently Asked About

Why Invest in Startups?

Everyone has their own reasons why they want to invest in a startup. It's likely a mix between making or growing their money and believing in the vision of a new company or idea!

Types of Investments

Three main types of investments offered on Invstme

1. Equity Investments: invest an amount of moey into a startup in exchange for a percentage of the company.

2. Debt Investments: invest in a company by loaning them money with the intention on collecting the loan plus interest at a later date.

3. Bespoke Loan Investment: invest in a company by loaning them money for whatever they intend on purchasing. In the event of the startup failing, the items bought using a particular individual's investment will be liquidated and the outstanding loan paid back. 

How to Decrease Your Risk?

1. Don't invest any money you cannot afford to loose.

2. Diversify your investments: invest smaller amounts of money in numerous companies rather than a large amount in just one company.

3. Focus your investments in areas you have experience in or are familiar with.

4. Read a startup's business plan. See if you believe it makes sense & agree with it.

Ease of Investment Resale

- As of this moment, there is no secondary market for equity in private companies.
- You will be able to sell your investment back to the company if you so choose.
- As the value of the company increases due to sale of equity you will be able to resell it to either the company or another buyer through Invstme. 

Dilution of Ownership Percentage

As a startup goes through multiple series of financing (till it reaches a stage for an IPO) the equity stake you own as an investor will be diluted.
 
This is normal and so long as the value of a company increases with each subsequent round it will not  negatively impact the value/worth of your investment.

Do Investors Receive Voting Rights?

It is unlikely that your investment will allow for voting rights. Although depending on the startup you decide to invest in, it may be a possibility. 

Though not all startups will offer voting rights, all investors on the Invstme platform have the ability to contact the company they are investing in. Any constructive criticism, opinions and ideas will be shared and heard. 

How to Choose What Startups to Fund?

Invstme is a platform that connects investors with entrepreneurs. We do not recommend any startups on the site. All startups that are featured on the site have been processed through our due diligence system. We recommend all prospective investors conduct their own due diligence.

All information presented on Invstme is provided by the startup companies themselves. 

How to Conduct Due Diligence?

As an investor, you are responsible for conducting due diligence on any startup you intend on investing in. 
Here at Invstme we make it easy to ask founders any & all questions you want before investing. Take advantage of this and ask the founders anything regarding their business. All of them are passionate about what they do and will gladly answer you! If you like what you hear, invest in them and their cause. If you don't like what you hear, then don't invest in them.

What Happens When I Invest?

Once you decide on investing your money in a startup your investment will be placed in an escrow account at the Royal Bank of Canada. Only once the fundraising has been completed will the funds be transferred to the startup's corporate account.

How is Invstme Compensated?

Invstme does not charge the investor a fee for their investment. The startup is charged a percentage ranging from 3.5% to 5% (depending on the criteria they fit into) of their total funding amount by Invstme plus a 5% credit card processing fee.

Cancelling an Investment

Investors have the ability to change their minds at any time before the deadline of the investing round. There doesn't need to be any reason behind the cancellation.

What Happens When a Funding Round Fails?

If an investment round does not meet it's funding goals, one of three things will happen.

1. If less than 60% of the goal was reached, the investor will have their total investment amount returned by Invstme.

2. If more than 61% of their goal was reached, all investors will receive an email asking whether they would still like to invest their original amount of money for an equity investment or whether they would like their money returned.

3. If a fundraising round fails, investors will be notified by email. They will be given an option to have their funds returned to them or instead be offered more favourable terms by the startup in question. This usually ends up with the startup offering to convert the investment amount into a Bespoke Loan Investment resulting in a more secure investment or perhaps a higher equity percentage for the same amount of money. This varies from one startup to another.

Staying in the Loop

Invstme requests and encourages startups featured on the platform to update investors every fiscal quarter. Although this is not a law, we suggest startups keep their investors updated once a month with a simple letter informing them on the goals, achievements and other statistics about the company.

Access to Founders

Invstme does not release any personal contact information about the founders. All communication must pass through the Invstme site.

Assisting Startups

Investors can help startups in numerous ways. Through the Invstme site you can offer your services, offer suggestions, pre-order products and more.

How Do I Earn a Return?

Returns depend on the type of investment you made.

1. All startups on the Invstme platform use a SAFE, this is an agreement that allows investors the right to obtain a piece of equity in the company at a later date when the startup sells it’s shares in a future financing round.
2. Some investors choose to invest by loaning a startup money in return for their initial investment + interest at an agreed upon later date. In this situation, the investor will be paid back their money + interest at the agreed upon date.

Some Topics We Are Frequently Asked About

What is Invstme?

Invstme is a platform that assists startups & businesses acquire the necessary funding to grow. The purpose is for us to raise funds and advertise for companies so you as an innovator can focus on growing your business and changing the world for the better! 

How is Invstme Different from Kickstarter?

Invstme wants customers to have a more integral role in your company. With Invstme, customers can own a percentage of your company thereby having a vested interest in it’s success. Kickstarter is one of the best sites out there for entrepreneurs at the moment! We think Kickstarter is fantastic, but Kickstarter is for finished products. Invstme allows you to sell convertible debt, equity and or Bespoke Loan Investments rather than just a finished product. We’re here for you from the day you conceive an idea till the day you deliver it. Invstme is all about building customer relations! What better way to keep your customers than to have them be investors with you as well.

Why was Invstme Created?

Invstme was created because of a need for it. We met with many bright and motivated fellow entrepreneurs who were not able to raise the necessary funds to start up a business. Startups are risky, the more innovative an idea the riskier it is. The world needs change and we at Invstme would like to assist in this process. At Invstme we applaud those who have ideas and are willing to help shape the world into a better place for all.
Similarly to the entrepreneur side of the story, we did not understand why it was illegal to invest in businesses just because of not having the necessary wealth. The venture capitalist and bank models are risk averse. This leads to only a small amount of startup ideas receiving funding. So we decided to make a site that allows anyone with an interest in investing, startups, business or a product to have the ability to invest in it!

How to Use Invstme to Raise Funds? 

1. Start by making a solid business plan for investors to view and decide whether they would like to invest in your company.

2. Decide what you want to get out of a series round & what you are willing to offer in return.
a) Sell equity
b) Sell debt
c) Sell a finished product
d) Recieve publicity & get your name/idea out
e) Recieve mentorship from individuals who believe in your idea and are willing to invest in you

3. Reach out to your followers & supporters. Let them know about your campaign

When Are You Ready For A Fundraising Round on Invstme?

Companies at any stage are able to raise funds on Invstme. The only stipulation we have is that a startup must be incorporated before applying. This is to ensure you are legaly able to sell equity in the company.
We do recommend companies have at least a working proof of concept for a project and a business model before starting a funding round but this is a guideline as we understand some ideas are too expensive start without funding (we’re looking at you companies that have your sights on Mars and beyond).
Ideally, companies attempting to raise funds on the Invstme Platform should have a following/customers who are interest in their product but this is not necessary. We offer services at Invstme to help startups get their name out and gain interest in their products.

What Businesses Can Be Funded?

Any and all types of incorporated businesses can be funded on the Invstme platform. We don’t pretend to know the whole market place, so if your business is funded we believe the market has spoken. At the very least, you have a niche market of customers and investors who believe in you!
The only businesses we do not raise funds for are those which perform illegal activities.

How Much Does it Cost to Fundraise on Invstme?

Invstme does not charge startups an upfront fee to start a funding round. Instead, we take a percentage of the total amount raised in a successful funding round.
At most, Invstme will take a 10% fee. The Breakdown is as follows: 5% Credit Card/Payment Processing Fee. 5% is Invstme’s fee percentage for most startups. 4% is the fee percentage for startups that are led by females and or startups that are endeavouring to make the world a better place (technologies that make the planet a greener place, reduction in poverty, increasing food production etc.).

Do All Investors Have Voting Rights?

Investors do not have legal voting rights but we do advise startup founders to heed the advice of investors should they have experience in your particular field.

How Does The Investment Process Work?

When an investor decides to invest their money in your company, they will sign a digital copy of the investment documents. Once both parties sign and accept the digital documents the investment money will be placed in an escrow account at the Royal Bank of Canada. After the funding round has been successfully completed the money will be transferred to your startup’s corporate account. 

Minimum & Maximum a Startup Can Raise Per Round

The minimum a startup can raise at Invstme is $1000.00 per campaign.
The maximum a startup can raise is $250,000 per campaign. 

How Much Work is Necessary For a Successful Round of Funding?

The more work you do to make your campaign a success, the higher the chances of it succeeding. We suggest you get your startup’s name out into the world. The more people know about it and hear about it the better your chances of a successful funding round. “Social Proof” is what gives strangers the confidence in investing their money in your company!
